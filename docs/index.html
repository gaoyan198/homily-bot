homily-bot methodology

A transparent approximation of how @dannycheng2022 uses Homily charts — long-term accumulation guidance, chip support/resistance, volatility holes and a stringent multi-bagger conviction screen. Every formula below is exactly what the code does; nothing proprietary, nothing hidden.

Read this first. Danny's real indicators are proprietary ("can never be duplicated" — his words) and his posted results are self-reported, unaudited and levered. This bot approximates his documented behaviour from public posts. Its own backtests are run honestly and two of them fail — those failures are kept in the daily digest on purpose. Nothing here is financial advice or a promise of returns.

1 · Philosophy (what we copied from Danny)

  • Never trade. Signals time adds on conviction names. There is no sell state anywhere in the system. The worst state, CAUTION, only pauses new buying.
  • A fixed watchlist, charted forever. The same names every day — holdings plus a curated universe — rather than chasing whatever moved yesterday.
  • Structure over candles. Levels come from where volume actually traded (chips) and where volatility collapsed (holes), not from chart patterns.
  • What we deliberately did NOT copy: leverage/margin (his $800k→$3.6M year used it), leveraged ETFs (SOXL), and crypto-beta names.

2 · The four signal engines

2.1 Weekly circle (trend regime)

Four weekly-close checks, one point each: price > 30-week EMA · 10-week EMA > 30-week EMA · MACD histogram > 0 with MACD line > 0 · 30-week SMA rising vs 4 weeks ago.

RED = 3–4  AMBER = 2  WHITE = 0–1

2.2 Chip distribution (筹码 — support / resistance / POC)

Each day's volume is spread triangularly across that day's high–low range, then decayed with a 60-trading-day half-life so recent accumulation dominates (the "dynamic POC" behaviour Danny describes; true chip models decay by turnover against float, which isn't available key-free).

  • POC — heaviest price bin: the crowd's cost basis.
  • Support — biggest chip peaks at/below price (holders in profit defend their basis). The top one becomes the add zone: peak −2% to +3%.
  • Resistance — peaks above price (trapped holders sell into rallies).
  • % in profit — share of chip weight below the close.

2.3 Volatility hole (Danny: "the most crucial part")

A hole day = 5-day average-true-range ÷ close makes a new 60-day low. Consecutive hole days (gaps ≤ 3) form a cluster; the cluster's high/low is the zone; it stays "in force" ≤ 90 bars. Judged off the latest close: ↑ BREAKOUT above the upper boundary, ↓ BREAKDOWN below the lower, ◻ INSIDE otherwise.

5 · Data & plumbing

  • Daily OHLCV: Yahoo v8 chart API, key-free, 5y range; weekly/monthly resampled from daily. Pure Python stdlib — no pip, no server.
  • Runs 09:00 SGT Mon–Fri on GitHub Actions; digest via Telegram bot API; auto-refine state committed back to the repo each run.
  • The weekly-circle parameters self-refine daily but a challenger is only adopted if it beats the champion out-of-sample (walk-forward), never on in-sample fit.
  • Self-tests (homily_validate.py) assert EMA math, no look-ahead, chip POC placement, decay behaviour, hole detection and composite states on synthetic data — run in CI every day.

6 · Known limitations

  • Dollar-volume ≠ market cap; hot names look "bigger" than they are.
  • No fundamentals: revenue growth, margins, dilution, insider activity are all invisible to this screen.
  • Chip decay uses a fixed half-life, not true float turnover.
  • Survivorship: the universe is hand-picked liquid names; the backtests run on today's list.
  • The conviction score is un-backtested (see §4) — stringency is a design choice, not yet a proven edge.
