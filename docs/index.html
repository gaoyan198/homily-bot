homily-bot methodology

A transparent approximation of how @dannycheng2022 uses Homily charts — long-term accumulation guidance, chip support/resistance, volatility holes and a stringent multi-bagger conviction screen. Every formula below is exactly what the code does; nothing proprietary, nothing hidden.

Read this first. Danny's real indicators are proprietary ("can never be duplicated" — his words) and his posted results are self-reported, unaudited and levered. This bot approximates his documented behaviour from public posts. Its own backtests are run honestly and two of them fail — those failures are kept in the daily digest on purpose. Nothing here is financial advice or a promise of returns.

1 · Philosophy (what we copied from Danny)

  • Never trade. Signals time adds on conviction names. There is no sell state anywhere in the system. The worst state, CAUTION, only pauses new buying.
  • A fixed watchlist, charted forever. The same names every day — holdings plus a curated universe — rather than chasing whatever moved yesterday.
  • Structure over candles. Levels come from where volume actually traded (chips) and where volatility collapsed (holes), not from chart patterns.
  • What we deliberately did NOT copy: leverage/margin (his $800k→$3.6M year used it), leveraged ETFs (SOXL), and crypto-beta names.

2 · The four signal engines

2.1 Weekly circle (trend regime)

Four weekly-close checks, one point each: price > 30-week EMA · 10-week EMA > 30-week EMA · MACD histogram > 0 with MACD line > 0 · 30-week SMA rising vs 4 weeks ago.

RED = 3–4  AMBER = 2  WHITE = 0–1

2.2 Chip distribution (筹码 — support / resistance / POC)

Each day's volume is spread triangularly across that day's high–low range, then decayed with a 60-trading-day half-life so recent accumulation dominates (the "dynamic POC" behaviour Danny describes; true chip models decay by turnover against float, which isn't available key-free).

  • POC — heaviest price bin: the crowd's cost basis.
  • Support — biggest chip peaks at/below price (holders in profit defend their basis). The top one becomes the add zone: peak −2% to +3%.
  • Resistance — peaks above price (trapped holders sell into rallies).
  • % in profit — share of chip weight below the close.

2.3 Volatility hole (Danny: "the most crucial part")

A hole day = 5-day average-true-range ÷ close makes a new 60-day low. Consecutive hole days (gaps ≤ 3) form a cluster; the cluster's high/low is the zone; it stays "in force" ≤ 90 bars. Judged off the latest close: ↑ BREAKOUT above the upper boundary, ↓ BREAKDOWN below the lower, ◻ INSIDE otherwise.

2.5 Whale-accumulation footprint (🐳) and the WHALE-DIP tier

Born from the PLTR June-2026 case: Danny added aggressively at the 113–119 chip shelf during the June 22–26 dip to 106 while the composite said ⚪ — the level agreed, the trend gate blocked the add, and the bounce proved him right. Homily's "main force" cost line is proprietary, so homily_whale.py approximates the footprint big buyers leave in public OHLCV. 🐳 requires an actual dip (close ≥5% below the 60-day closing high) plus at least 2 of 3:

  • Absorption print — a ≥1.3×-average-volume day probing within 3% of the 20-day min low yet closing in the top half of its range (PLTR 2026-06-26: 1.44× volume, closed at 0.8 of the range, before the July-1 rip to 125);
  • Flow divergence — OBV or the accumulation/distribution line at/above its pre-dip-peak level while price is down (money in, price down);
  • Shelf stability — the decayed chip weight in the ±2% band around the support shelf fully replenished over the last 10 bars while price sits on it (holders absorbing, not fleeing).

The shipping gate (homily_whale_backtest.py, 58 names = current universe + the 2021 hype control, 5y point-in-time, $1 per qualifying day) passed: ⚪ dips at the shelf WITH 🐳 returned +3.5% fwd-20d / +10.9% fwd-60d vs the +3.1% / +9.5% DCA baseline and +3.2% / +9.7% unconditioned ⚪ dips — and held on the 2021 control alone (+8.1% vs +5.6% fwd-60d). Hence the promoted rule: ⚪ + 🎯 + 🐳 = WHALE-DIP tier, the one case a ⚪ name may be added — discretionary, ≤2% of account per name, out of the same monthly budget, 10%/name hard cap. Honesty: the edge is modest (+1.4pts fwd-60d), qualifying days cluster (~6 per episode, 744 episodes), one 5y window — and the shelf alone (🎯 without 🐳) lost to plain dips: the footprint, not the level, carries the edge.

4 · When to SELL — the market regime signal

Per-stock selling failed our backtests twice, but index-level regime timing is the one trend rule with a long honest record: the classic 10-month SMA rule. At each month end, if the index closes below its 10-month simple moving average → BEAR; above → BULL. It is slow on purpose — it fires a handful of times a decade, which is what makes it decisive rather than noisy.

RegimeCondition (month-end closes)Protocol
🐂 BULL SPY and QQQ above their 10m SMA stay invested, add via ⭐/🚀 screens
⚖️ MIXED one above, one below no action — judge at the next month-end
🐻 BEAR SPY and QQQ below their 10m SMA the decisive sell: halt adds, exit satellite/🚀 positions, raise dry powder; long-horizon index core stays; re-enter when a month-end close regains the SMA

5 · THE test — full strategy vs DCA into SPY / QQQ

The question that matters: does "accumulate on ⭐ dips across the universe, sell everything on 🐻, redeploy on 🐂" beat the same money mechanically DCA'd into an index? homily_strategy_backtest.py replays it point-in-time, monthly, no look-ahead, 10bps per trade, $1/month, July 2021 → July 2026 — a window that contains the 2021 top, the 2022 bear and the 2023–25 bull.

Two universes, because which list you feed the engine decides the result: universe A is today's bot list — hindsight-picked 2026 winners, upward-biased by construction. Universe B is the honest control: what a growth investor plausibly held in mid-2021, winners and still-listed wrecks (PTON, ZM, DOCU, ROKU, LCID, TDOC, BYND, UPST…). Fully delisted names can't be fetched key-free, so some survivorship remains even in B.

Hard-number verdict, bias removed.
  • The accumulation engine earned its keep on the control: 2.10× vs QQQ's 1.74× and SPY's 1.50×, with a smaller drawdown than QQQ — on a basket deliberately salted with 2021 wrecks. The trend+support gates (monthly UP · weekly RED · chip support) largely refused to keep feeding PTON/ZM/DOCU-style death spirals. Genuinely encouraging, but it is ONE 5-year window and residual survivorship remains — call it promising, not proven.
  • The 🐻 regime-sell overlay was pure cost in this window: it roughly halved returns on both universes (A: 3.69→1.69; B: 2.10→1.31, losing to SPY) while improving drawdown by ~1 point, because 2022 was followed by a V-recovery the monthly signal re-entered late, and dip-gated buying already avoids tops. Reconcile with §4: over 33 years the regime rule pays for itself in dot-com/GFC-scale wrecks. It is catastrophe insurance — in windows without a catastrophe, insurance shows up only as premium.
  • Net recommendation the data supports: run the ⭐ accumulation engine as the buying discipline; treat the 🐻 signal as a de-risking trigger for satellite positions and new-buying pauses, not a full portfolio liquidation; keep the index core permanently invested.

5b · Danny-style concentration — 90% of the book in a top-4 core

The executable version for a full-time working adult: $1/month split across four core names, five minutes a month (homily_core4_backtest.py, July 2021 → July 2026, 10bps, no look-ahead). Three cores tested: Danny's literal four (NVDA PLTR AMD HOOD — pure hindsight: in July 2021 HOOD was a fresh meme-IPO), an engine-picked four chosen once in 2021 by point-in-time conviction score over the loser-salted control pool, and the realistic version — re-picked every July, one decision a year.

Correction: Danny's concentration is emergent, not picked

He never selected a core-4 — he buys dips only while a name's trend is intact, never sells in a bull market, and the compounders grow into the core. homily_emergent_backtest.py replays exactly that mechanic on the control universe and measures what concentration appears by itself:

  • Concentration emerged exactly as described: from 27 names ever bought, PLTR compounded into ~30% of the final book, top-4 into 62% (and rising — Danny's 85/90% is what this looks like after a decade, not five years). Winners built the core; the trend gate quietly stopped feeding the losers.
  • Emergent beat engineered: same return as the flat ⭐ strategy (it IS the ⭐ strategy) with −30% drawdown, vs −68% for the forced annual re-pick core-4 — because the losers were never given 22.5% of the book to destroy.
  • Conviction-weighting the adds changed nothing (2.09 vs 2.10): the ⭐ state gate does all the work; sizing refinement within it is noise. Skip the complexity.

6 · Data & plumbing

  • Daily OHLCV: Yahoo v8 chart API, key-free, 5y range; weekly/monthly resampled from daily. Pure Python stdlib — no pip, no server.
  • Runs 09:00 SGT Mon–Fri on GitHub Actions; digest via Telegram bot API; auto-refine state committed back to the repo each run.
  • The weekly-circle parameters self-refine daily but a challenger is only adopted if it beats the champion out-of-sample (walk-forward), never on in-sample fit.
  • Self-tests (homily_validate.py) assert EMA math, no look-ahead, chip POC placement, decay behaviour, hole detection and composite states on synthetic data — run in CI every day.

7 · Known limitations

  • Dollar-volume ≠ market cap; hot names look "bigger" than they are.
  • Fundamentals are informational only: the F:n/m tag on 🚀/discovery rows (EDGAR: revenue growth >10% · profit or positive operating cash flow · dilution <12%/yr) never gates timing — by design, per the lag argument — so a tape-only trap with rotten financials still prints its signal, just with a visible F:0/3. Margins and insider activity remain invisible.
  • Chip decay uses a fixed half-life, not true float turnover.
  • Survivorship: the universe is hand-picked liquid names; the backtests run on today's list.
  • The conviction score is un-backtested (see §4) — stringency is a design choice, not yet a proven edge.
